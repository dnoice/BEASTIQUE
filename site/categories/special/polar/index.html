Polar Beasts

Life at the Edge of the Thaw

Animals engineered for a world of ice, watching it melt beneath them.

Descend

“

The poles are the planet's cold engine, and the animals built there are built for a temperature we are switching off. White coats, blubbered bodies, blood that runs in the freeze — exquisite engineering for a habitat with a shrinking floor.

The ice is the ground, and the ground is disappearing. The bear needs the pack to hunt; the narwhal needs the leads to breathe; the whole architecture is keyed to a season that arrives later and leaves sooner every year. This is extraction at a distance — the carbon burned elsewhere, the bill paid here.

BEASTIQUE renders them in the materials of a colder value — pewter and silver for the animal fixed in place while its world runs out from under it. Beauty is the argument. This is what the thaw is spending.
