Blood of the Wolf

“

Every dog asleep at a human hearth is a wolf that made a bargain. This hall gathers the ones who refused it — the wild canids, the lineage that stayed feral while its cousins were bred into the couch.

We repaid the refusal with a bounty. Wolves poisoned as competition, wild dogs shot as vermin, foxes run for sport and fur — the most social, most intelligent predators on the continent persecuted for the crime of hunting the same prey we do.

BEASTIQUE renders them in the materials of that grudge — weathered bronze for the animal we cast as villain and then mounted on a wall. Beauty is the argument. This is the family portrait we tried not to keep.
