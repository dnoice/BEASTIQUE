The Invisible Architecture of Life

The load-bearing wall of the living world, treated as if it were nothing.

“

Insects are the architecture beneath everything. They pollinate the crop, turn the soil, feed the chain, and carry the dead back into the ground. Pull the thread and the tapestry does not fray — it drops.

We are pulling the thread. Monocultures, broad-spectrum pesticide, and a night sky we refuse to switch off are collapsing insect biomass at a rate that outpaces almost every vertebrate. The monarch crosses a continent on wings thinner than paper and lands in a forest already sold for timber.

BEASTIQUE renders them in the materials that make their loss legible — cut crystal for the pollinator we treat as decorative until the harvest fails. Beauty is the argument. This is the earthquake under the field.
