The ocean was the first cathedral. Every living thing that ever crawled onto land carried the sea back out of it — in blood, in tears, in the salt of every cell.

We are draining the cathedral. The beluga sturgeon has swum since before the dinosaurs; we grind its roe into caviar and price it by the gram. The vaquita — fewer than ten remain on Earth — drowns in nets set for a fish it never was. What the trawler misses, the warming water takes.

BEASTIQUE renders them in the materials of that transaction — steel for the apex hunter reduced to a fin on a scale, silver for the body sold by weight. The beauty is the receipt. This is what the water is spending.
