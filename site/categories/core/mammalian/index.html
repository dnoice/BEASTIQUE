We are the mammals who learned to price the other mammals. The megafauna we share this planet with are vanishing at a rate not seen since the ice age ended — and this time the glacier is us.

Poaching, fragmentation, and a destabilised climate are erasing lineages that took millions of years to evolve. The leopard is worth more as a coat than as a predator; the range that fed it is worth more as pasture. Every extinction is a door that does not reopen.

BEASTIQUE renders them in the materials of that exchange — gold for the animal reduced to a trophy, a pelt, a line item. Beauty is the argument. This is the closing entry.
