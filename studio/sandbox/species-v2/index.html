About the Species

The World's Rarest
Big Cat

The Amur leopard once roamed widely across the Russian Far East, northeast China, and the Korean Peninsula. Now it has been cornered into a final refuge — the snow-dusted temperate forests where Russia meets China.

Each individual's rosette pattern is unique — a natural fingerprint that allows researchers to identify and track every surviving leopard. With fewer than 120 remaining, every single cat is known.

Its thick winter coat — growing up to 7cm — is adapted for temperatures dropping to -30°C. The pale, cream-colored winter pelage is among the most beautiful in the animal kingdom, and tragically, the most coveted.
